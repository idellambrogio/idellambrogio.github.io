Presentation

The goal of this course is to introduce the fundamental concepts and tools of algebraic topology. The focus will be on the combinatorial aspects of homological constructions.

This course will enable students to follow in the second term (the 4th and last term of "Master Mathématiques Recherche") the advanced course by Alexis Virelizier on topological quantum field theories.

We will begin with classical notions such as the triangulation of surfaces and manifolds. Then we will introduce singular homology theory, which will enable us to prove the topological invariance of previously defined combinatorial expressions. We will provide a complete presentation both of the foundations of the theory and of its classical applications: the Brouwer fixed-point theorem, the Jordan curve theorem, the invariance of domain, etc. We will then treat the Poincaré duality theorem. In the last part of the course we will review the combinatorial approach by studying the notion of simplicial complex, which models the underlying combinatorics of polyhedra. This will lead us to the simplicial interpretation of Poincaré duality.

Course planning

§1. Introduction : triangulation and classification of surfaces

  • Definition of manifold, surface, orientation
  • Triangulation of compact surfaces
  • Construction of surfaces from polygons
  • Euler characteristic, the problem of topological invariance
  • Higher dimensions and the Hauptvermutung

§2. The homology of spaces

  • Axiomatic properties of homology
  • Examples of computations: surfaces, spheres, projective spaces, ...
  • Applications : the theorems of Brouwer, Jordan, Borsuk-Ulam, invariance of domain, hairy ball, ...
  • Definition of singular homology
  • Homology of complexes and homological algebra
  • Properties of singular homology
  • Torsion groups and the universal coefficient formula for homology

§3. Duality and pseudo-manifolds

  • Cohomology
  • Cup-product and cap-product
  • Fundamental classes and the Poincaré duality theorem

§4. Simplicial complexes and homology

  • Simplicial complexes: abstract definition, geometric realization
  • Simplicial homology
  • The agreement of simplicial and singular homology

Prerequisites:

Fundamental notions of point-set topology (topological space, quotients, push-outs, etc.) and algebra (rings and modules).
